# Opening Chapter Checklist

**Purpose:** Chapter 1 is the single most consequential chapter of any book. It must establish more in fewer words than any other chapter. This checklist applies extra scrutiny beyond the standard revision checklist.

Run this after drafting Chapter 1, before proceeding to Chapter 2.

---

## 🎯 The Reader Promise Test

The first chapter makes an implicit contract with the reader about what kind of book this is. Check:

- [ ] **Genre tone is established within the first page** — the reader knows what kind of story they're in
- [ ] **The emotional register is set** — (e.g., melancholic, tense, playful) and consistent with the style guide
- [ ] **The point-of-view character is clearly identified** within the first 3 paragraphs
- [ ] **One central question or tension is opened** that the reader wants to see resolved
- [ ] **The world's rules are implied**, not info-dumped — the reader should feel them, not read a description

---

## 🪝 The Hook Check

- [ ] **Line 1 earns its place** — it does not start with weather, waking up, or description
- [ ] **Page 1 ends with a pull** — something that makes not reading page 2 feel uncomfortable
- [ ] **The first scene creates immediate tension** — even a quiet scene should contain conflict or unease

---

## 👤 Protagonist Establishment

- [ ] **We understand who this person is** from their actions and speech, not from description
- [ ] **Their dominant desire or wound is implied** — not stated outright, but felt
- [ ] **Their voice is distinct** — if the dialogue were unattributed, we'd know it was this character
- [ ] **We care about what happens to them** — even if we don't yet like them

---

## 🔗 Setup for Payoffs

- [ ] **Any foreshadowed element can be paid off** — nothing introduced that cannot eventually matter
- [ ] **No chekhov's gun left uncocked** — if a detail is planted, it must have a purpose
- [ ] **The thematic question of the book is seeded** — not stated, but present beneath the surface

---

## 🗣️ Exposition & Worldbuilding

- [ ] **No information dump in the first 3 pages** — setting, world, and history are delivered through action
- [ ] **Character backstory is implied, not explained** — what the character notices tells us who they are
- [ ] **The reader can orient themselves without a glossary** — if they can't, consider restructuring

---

## ✅ Final Gate

Before passing Chapter 1:
- [ ] Read the first and last lines aloud. Do they feel like the same book?
- [ ] Would a reader who knows nothing about this story want to read Chapter 2?
- [ ] Does the chapter deliver on the genre's core emotional promise (for thriller: dread; for romance: longing; for literary: insight)?
